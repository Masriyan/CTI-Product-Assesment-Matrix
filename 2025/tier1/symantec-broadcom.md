# Symantec/Broadcom Threat Intelligence

## Overview
Symantec Threat Intelligence, now part of Broadcom's enterprise security division, leverages one of the world's largest civilian cyber intelligence networks. With decades of security research heritage and telemetry from millions of endpoints globally, Symantec provides comprehensive threat intelligence integrated across its enterprise security portfolio.

## Key Features
- Global Intelligence Network with data from 175+ million endpoints
- Threat intelligence from 157 countries
- DeepSight Threat Intelligence platform
- Email threat intelligence and spam data
- WebPulse web reputation and URL categorization
- Advanced Threat Research (ATR) team intelligence
- Malware analysis and reverse engineering
- Vulnerability and exploit intelligence
- Targeted attack analytics
- MITRE ATT&CK mapped intelligence

## Pros
- Massive global telemetry from one of the largest sensor networks
- Decades of threat research and malware analysis experience
- Excellent email threat intelligence and anti-spam data
- Strong web reputation and URL filtering database
- Good coverage of targeted attacks and APTs
- Integration with comprehensive Symantec security portfolio
- Regular threat intelligence reports and analysis
- Strong file reputation database from Norton ecosystem
- Good vulnerability intelligence and threat assessment
- Established presence in enterprise and government sectors

## Cons
- Organizational changes under Broadcom ownership
- Platform modernization lagging behind newer competitors
- Best value within Broadcom/Symantec product ecosystem
- Third-party integration capabilities limited
- Some uncertainty around product roadmap post-acquisition
- Separate products and consoles across portfolio
- Commercial threat intelligence API less robust than dedicated platforms
- Customer support concerns reported post-Broadcom acquisition

## Use Cases
- Organizations using Symantec/Broadcom security products
- Enterprise endpoint protection and EDR
- Email security and anti-spam operations
- Web security and URL filtering
- DLP and data protection programs
- Large enterprise and government security operations
- Managed security service providers (MSSPs)
- Vulnerability management programs

## Integration Capabilities
- Native integration across Symantec enterprise security suite (SEP, EDR, Email Security, DLP)
- Integrated Cyber Defense (ICD) platform integration
- Symantec Endpoint Protection Manager (SEPM)
- Limited SIEM integration (primarily through logs)
- Third-party TIP integration (limited)
- RESTful APIs for DeepSight intelligence
- STIX/TAXII support (limited)
- Symantec Messaging Gateway integration

## Pricing Model
- Subscription-based licensing
- Per-endpoint, per-user, or per-server pricing
- DeepSight Intelligence subscription (separate)
- Typically bundled with Symantec security products
- Annual or multi-year contracts
- Enterprise licensing agreements
- Custom pricing for large deployments
- Additional costs for premium intelligence services

## Target Organization Size
- Mid-market to Large Enterprise
- Government and critical infrastructure
- Organizations with 1,000+ employees
- Established enterprises with legacy Symantec deployments
