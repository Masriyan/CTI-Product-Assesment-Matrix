# EclecticIQ Intelligence Center

## Overview
EclecticIQ Intelligence Center is a Threat Intelligence Platform designed for analyst-driven intelligence operations. Founded in 2014 in the Netherlands, EclecticIQ focuses on enabling intelligence analysts to collect, process, analyze, and disseminate threat intelligence using structured analytical techniques and collaborative workflows.

## Key Features
- Analyst-centric threat intelligence platform
- Structured analytical techniques and intelligence cycle support
- Threat intelligence fusion and correlation
- MITRE ATT&CK framework integration
- Intelligence production and dissemination
- Collaborative intelligence workflows
- Graph-based visualization and analysis
- Outgoing feeds to defensive systems
- Intelligence requirements management
- STIX 2.1 native support

## Pros
- Strong focus on intelligence analyst workflows
- Excellent support for structured analytical techniques
- Good for building mature threat intelligence programs
- Native STIX 2.1 support and standards compliance
- Flexible and customizable platform
- Open-source integrations and community edition
- Good collaboration and team features
- Regular updates and feature development
- Strong in European market
- Good for government and defense sectors

## Cons
- Requires experienced intelligence analysts to maximize value
- Smaller market presence than US-based competitors
- Integration ecosystem less extensive than major platforms
- Steeper learning curve for non-analysts
- Limited out-of-the-box threat intelligence feeds
- Best suited for organizations building custom intel programs
- May be overkill for small SOC teams

## Use Cases
- Analyst-driven threat intelligence operations
- Government and defense intelligence programs
- Strategic threat intelligence production
- Intelligence fusion centers
- Collaborative intelligence sharing (ISACs, CERTs)
- Structured analytical technique application
- Custom threat intelligence program development
- Research and academic institutions

## Integration Capabilities
- SIEM integrations (Splunk, Microsoft Sentinel, ELK)
- SOAR platforms (limited)
- MISP and OpenCTI integration
- Defensive security tool integration (IDS/IPS, firewalls)
- RESTful API
- STIX/TAXII server and client (native STIX 2.1)
- YARA rule distribution
- Integration with commercial and open-source feeds

## Pricing Model
- Subscription-based annual licensing
- Tiered pricing (Community, Professional, Enterprise)
- Per-analyst pricing model
- Community edition available (limited features)
- Custom enterprise pricing
- Professional services for implementation
- Training and certification programs
- Pricing typically $40,000+ annually for commercial editions

## Target Organization Size
- Mid-market to Large Enterprise
- Government and defense organizations
- Organizations with 500+ employees
- Best for teams with dedicated threat intelligence analysts
- Strong in European and government sectors
