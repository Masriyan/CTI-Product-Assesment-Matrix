# Intel 471 TITAN

## Overview
Intel 471 specializes in cybercrime intelligence, focusing on monitoring underground communities, dark web marketplaces, and threat actor communications. Founded by former intelligence community professionals, Intel 471 provides deep insights into cybercriminal operations, malware development, and emerging threats from the criminal underground.

## Key Features
- Deep and dark web monitoring
- Cybercrime actor tracking and profiling
- Malware intelligence and analysis
- Ransomware gang monitoring
- Underground marketplace monitoring
- Initial access broker (IAB) tracking
- Vulnerability exploit intelligence
- TITAN platform for intelligence delivery
- Information stealer and botnet tracking
- Adversary infrastructure monitoring

## Pros
- Exceptional deep and dark web intelligence
- Human intelligence (HUMINT) from underground sources
- Strong focus on financially motivated cybercrime
- Excellent ransomware and malware family tracking
- Early warning of emerging threats and exploits
- High-fidelity intelligence with context
- Good coverage of initial access brokers and RaaS
- Detailed threat actor profiling
- Proactive intelligence on planned attacks
- Strong analyst support and reporting

## Cons
- Premium pricing reflects specialized intelligence
- Narrow focus on cybercrime (limited nation-state coverage)
- Smaller customer base than major platforms
- Limited integration ecosystem
- Best suited for organizations facing cybercrime threats
- Requires analyst expertise to fully leverage
- Less tactical IOC feeds compared to broader platforms

## Use Cases
- Financial services and banking security
- Ransomware threat monitoring and prevention
- Brand protection and fraud prevention
- Third-party and supply chain risk assessment
- Incident response and threat hunting
- Malware analysis and reverse engineering
- Initial access broker monitoring
- Credential compromise monitoring

## Integration Capabilities
- SIEM integrations (limited)
- Threat intelligence platforms (ThreatConnect, Anomali, MISP)
- RESTful API for custom integrations
- STIX/TAXII feeds
- Slack and collaboration tools for alerts
- Email and webhook notifications
- Limited native security product integrations

## Pricing Model
- Subscription-based annual licensing
- Custom pricing based on organization size and needs
- TITAN platform access included
- Additional costs for analyst support and custom reporting
- Premium pricing tier ($100,000+ annually typical)
- Multi-year commitments may offer discounts

## Target Organization Size
- Mid-market to Large Enterprise
- Financial services and Fortune 500 companies
- Organizations with 1,000+ employees
- Best for organizations facing significant cybercrime threats
