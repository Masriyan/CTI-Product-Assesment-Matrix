# Bitsight

## Overview
Bitsight is a cybersecurity ratings and risk management platform that provides continuous security performance monitoring for organizations and their third parties. Founded in 2011, Bitsight pioneered the concept of security ratings, using external data to assess and quantify cybersecurity posture, helping organizations manage third-party risk, benchmark performance, and demonstrate security improvements to stakeholders.

## Key Features
- Security ratings (A-F scale)
- Third-party risk management (TPRM)
- Continuous external attack surface monitoring
- Industry and peer benchmarking
- Cyber insurance underwriting support
- Vendor risk assessment and monitoring
- Data breach likelihood prediction
- Executive and board reporting
- Supply chain risk intelligence
- Regulatory compliance monitoring

## Pros
- Industry-standard security ratings widely recognized
- Excellent third-party risk management capabilities
- Continuous monitoring without agent deployment
- Good for cyber insurance and underwriting
- Strong executive and board-level reporting
- Comprehensive vendor risk assessment
- Good industry benchmarking capabilities
- Integration with GRC platforms
- Strong in financial services and enterprises
- Objective, data-driven risk assessment

## Cons
- Ratings based on external data only (not internal controls)
- Can be expensive for comprehensive coverage
- Rating methodology sometimes disputed by rated organizations
- Limited tactical threat intelligence
- Best suited for risk management vs. operational security
- Integration with security tools (SIEM, SOAR) limited
- Some false positives in attack surface detection

## Use Cases
- Third-party and vendor risk management
- Cyber insurance underwriting and renewal
- Board and executive risk reporting
- Regulatory compliance (DORA, NYDFS, etc.)
- M&A due diligence
- Supply chain risk assessment
- Security posture benchmarking
- Cybersecurity performance measurement

## Integration Capabilities
- GRC platforms (ServiceNow GRC, OneTrust, Archer)
- Risk management systems
- Ticketing (ServiceNow, Jira)
- SIEM integration (limited)
- RESTful API
- Webhook notifications
- Business intelligence tools (PowerBI, Tableau)
- Integration primarily focused on risk/GRC vs. security operations

## Pricing Model
- Subscription-based annual licensing
- Tiered pricing based on organization size and features
- Pricing for monitoring own organization vs. third parties
- Custom enterprise pricing
- Typically $50,000-200,000+ annually
- Additional costs for vendor/third-party monitoring
- Volume discounts for large vendor portfolios

## Target Organization Size
- Mid-market to Large Enterprise
- Organizations with 500+ employees
- Financial services and regulated industries
- Organizations with extensive supply chains
